Drug Repurposing: Targeting mTOR Inhibitors for Anticancer Activity

In the search of safer and more effective drugs while reducing costs and increasing productivity of novel drug discovery, scientists are changing their focus to an approach known as drug repurposing. This involves finding a new therapeutic effect of an already existing drug. It is a method that can effectively be addressed in the drug discovery and development challenges of targeting different disorders.

In Silico Structure Based Designing of Dihydrofolate Reductase Enzyme Antagonists

In this study, Human DHFR obtained from Protein Data Bank (PDB) docked with several established anticancer drugs including Afatinib, Doxorubicin, Trimetrexate, Curcumin & Trimethoprim and several potential small molecules including Acarbose, Adenosine monophosphate, Abacavir, Aceprometazine & Isoxyl; obtained from PubChem and Drug Bank respectively. PyMOL and PyRx were used to visualize, curate and dock.
